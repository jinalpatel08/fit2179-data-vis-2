01 — Temperature

Australia is heating up

Since 1910, Australia's average temperature has risen by approximately 1.5°C. The warming has accelerated markedly since the 1950s, with the most recent decade being the hottest on record.

02 — By State

Where is it warming fastest?

South Australia and the ACT have experienced the sharpest temperature rises, while Tasmania has warmed the least. Inland states consistently show higher anomalies than coastal regions.

03 — Rainfall

The rains are shifting — and shrinking

Australia's rainfall is highly variable year to year, but the long-term trend shows increasing drought frequency. 2019 was the driest year since records began in 1900, with just 275mm — well below the long-term average of around 465mm.

04 — Water Storage

Melbourne's reservoirs under pressure

Melbourne's water storage has fluctuated dramatically since 1948. The Millennium Drought (1997–2009) drove storage to a critical low of just 25.9% in 2009 — triggering permanent water restrictions and the construction of the Wonthaggi desalination plant.
