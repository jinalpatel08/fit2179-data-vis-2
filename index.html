Australia's Changing Climate

How rising temperatures, shifting rainfall, and worsening extremes are reshaping the continent — 1910 to today

Australia is heating up

Since 1910, Australia's average temperature has risen by approximately 1.5°C. The warming has accelerated since the 1950s, with the most recent decade being the hottest on record.

Where is it warming fastest?

South Australia and Victoria have experienced the sharpest temperature rises, while Tasmania has warmed the least. Inland states consistently show higher anomalies than coastal regions.

The rains are shifting — and shrinking

Australia's rainfall is highly variable year to year, but the long-term trend shows increasing drought frequency. 2019 was the driest year since records began in 1900, with just 275mm — well below the long-term average of around 465mm.
